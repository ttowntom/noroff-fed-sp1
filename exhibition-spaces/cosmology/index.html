About this space

Explore the wonders of our cosmos. Our fantastic exhibition, ‘The Sky Above Us’, explores the night sky and what we can see and know about the universe around us. We’ll locate the various constellations and galaxies that can be seen and learn a bit about the early navigators who used the stars to travel by.

Follow the journey of our solar exploration: from early Arab traders, to Galileo’s telescope, to the latest exploration of the planets in our solar system.

Did you know?

You can see back in time

The light you see from the stars might be thousands of years old, meaning you're glimpsing into the distant past as you gaze at the night sky.

Cosmic siblings

Our sun is just one star among billions in our Milky Way galaxy. And there are billions of galaxies in the observable universe!

Celestial ballet

Galaxies collide and merge, creating cosmic collisions that give birth to new stars and shape the universe's structure.

Black hole puzzles

Black holes are so massive that their gravity warps space and time, making them 'cosmic vacuum cleaners' that nothing can escape from.
